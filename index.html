Thoughts on Progressive Enhancement

Progressive enhancement is a strategy for web development that prioritizes the delivery of core content first. Instead of building for the most advanced browsers and hoping it "breaks gracefully" for others, you start with a rock-solid foundation of semantic HTML—the "peanut"—and then add layers of design (CSS) and interactivity (JavaScript) on top.

This approach is vital in modern web development because it ensures universal accessibility. In a world with thousands of different devices, screen sizes, and varying internet speeds, progressive enhancement guarantees that everyone can access the basic information they need, regardless of how powerful their technology is. It treats content as the most important part of the user experience, rather than just a byproduct of a fancy design.

To learn more about how this philosophy has evolved, I recommend reading: Understanding Progressive Enhancement (A List Apart) .
