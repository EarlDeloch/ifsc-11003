Scientific Breakthroughs

Each rover is a sophisticated mobile laboratory. From the first analysis of Martian rocks to the discovery of ancient organic molecules, these missions have redefined our understanding of the solar system.

Sojourner: Proving the Concept

Technical Features: Sojourner was equipped with an Alpha Proton X-ray Spectrometer (APXS) to determine the chemical composition of rocks and soil.

Major Discovery: The mission provided evidence that Mars was once "warm and wet," with liquid water once flowing across the surface to create the floodplains of Ares Vallis.

Curiosity: Searching for Habitability

Technical Features: Curiosity features the 'ChemCam,' a laser that vaporizes rocks to study their composition from a distance, and a full chemistry lab in its belly (SAM).

Major Discovery: Curiosity confirmed that Gale Crater once hosted a freshwater lake system that contained all the chemical ingredients necessary for microbial life.
